    Glossary

As with any sport, there are many terms that sound weird and unfamiliar to beginners. Learn proper usage of these words and you'll have a much easier time communicating with your fellow folfers.

  • Folf

    -- Combination of the words "Frisbee" and "Golf" used to refer to Disc Golf. Also used with other iterations such as "folfing" and "folfers."

  • Hole

    • -- A corresponding basket teepad and fairway, and the surrounding area.

  • Drive

    • -- The first throw on a hole. The objective is to close the majority of the distance between the teepad and the basket.

  • Teepad

    • -- The surface from which a drive is taken. There is one at the start of each hole.

  • Basket

    • -- The container that discs must rest in to complete the hole.

  • Fairway

    • -- The central or primary path between the teepad and basket.

  • FORE

    • -- This term is used to signal other folfers that a wayward disc is headed in their direction. Yell it loudly.

  • Clear

    • -- Referring to a hole that is not actively being played on. It is courteous to yell "Clear!" after completing and leaving a hole in order to signal the next group that the hole is available.
